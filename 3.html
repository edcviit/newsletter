    INTRODUCTION



    In 2002, Manikchand Oxyrich began near Pune, blossoming into a premium drinking water brand. With plants in Hyderabad, Bangalore, Ahmedabad, and Haridwar, their focus on quality extends to supplying essentials nationwide. Oxyrich revolutionizes healthy hydration across India.


    OXYRICH : A BRAND



    Oxyrich became a brand by tapping into the youth's thirst for success with the rallying cry “Pyaas Jagao”. Their outdoor campaign redefined 'thirst' as the drive to achieve, setting an industry trend seen in Limca's 'Pyaas Badhao.' Claiming to be the only Indian packaged water with 300% more oxygen, Oxyrich's patented technology makes it a market front-runner. Targeting a youth demographic into sports and fitness, Oxyrich's design exudes vitality, enhancing its appeal


    STRATEGIC REBRANDING AND ENHANCED AUTHENTICITY



    In response to the escalating number of counterfeit products flooding the market, Oxyrich took a pivotal step by initiating a rebranding strategy. The company identified the misuse of its iconic blue label by approximately 500 local units and promptly took decisive action to safeguard customer trust.


    In a bold move on September 5, 2023, Oxyrich transitioned from its traditional blue label to a vibrant red and white design, unveiling a novel logo and an aesthetically pleasing label. This transformation aims to prevent malpractice, eliminate label duplication, and ensure easy recognition of genuine Oxyrich products. Beyond safeguarding the brand's integrity, it serves as a deterrent to counterfeit enterprises.


    Oxyrich asserts its commitment to legal action against replication attempts. The rebranding event, hosted at the JW Marriott Hotel in Pune, marked a strategic milestone with the presence of distributors, franchisees, and entrepreneurs nationwide.


    EMOTION AND MARKETING


    The Manikchand Oxyrich Group seized the opportunity presented by Pune's grand Ganesh Utsav, strategically sponsoring select Ganesh mandals to engage with the vibrant celebration.


    Notably, the Shrimant Bhausaheb Rangari Ganapati and other trusts facilitated the placement of Oxyrich banners along the roadsides in Pune's old city area. Capitalizing on the festive fervor, Oxyrich leveraged social media influencers who creatively integrated the brand into memes, playfully highlighting the prevalence of Oxyrich banners in the city. Furthermore, Instagram reels showcased Oxyrich-themed decorations at the Akhil Mandai Mandal, fostering an emotional connection with the local populace.


    During the bustling 10 days of Ganesh Utsav, Manikchand Oxyrich strategically seized the essential need for water, positioning its brand prominently. In the midst of devotees' queues, Oxyrich dominated by ensuring its visibility through filled bottles with vendors and even empty ones in dustbins. This on-ground promotion, amid a digital era, showcased the brand's commitment to a tangible and widespread presence. The goal was to create the illusion of popularity, making Oxyrich the default refreshment option during the festivities.


    CONCLUSION



    This localized marketing model, though seemingly modest, serves as a microcosm for understanding the efficacy of larger-scale marketing strategies. Manikchand Oxyrich's astute approach during Ganesh Utsav exemplifies how strategic brand penetration can efficiently shape consumer preferences and drive market prominence.
